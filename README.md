The recent evolution in everyday language usage has significantly transformed the way people communicate in digital environments. With the widespread adoption of social media platforms, there has been a noticeable increase in the use of abbreviations, shorthand expressions, emojis, and hybrid words, leading to a deviation from traditional English vocabulary and grammar structures.

This study focuses on analyzing linguistic patterns in user-generated content collected from Twitter, a widely used social media platform where users express opinions and emotions in real time. The dataset consists of randomly sampled tweets written in English, making it suitable for unbiased linguistic analysis.

Natural Language Processing (NLP) techniques are applied to preprocess and analyze the textual data. The collected tweets are cleaned by removing noise such as URLs, hashtags, mentions, and numerical values. Further processing involves tokenization and stopword removal to extract meaningful words.

Each word in the dataset is categorized into two groups: Proper English Words, which belong to standard English vocabulary, and Improper English Words, which include informal, slang, or non-standard lexical forms. This classification helps in understanding the extent of deviation from standard English usage in social media communication.

The study provides insights into modern linguistic trends and highlights the impact of digital communication on language evolution.
